Abstract

Contemporary climate discourse is overwhelmingly focused on carbon dioxide reduction as the primary mitigation strategy for global warming. However, this approach fundamentally misunderstands the true nature of planetary collapse. The failure mechanism threatening global civilization is not atmospheric CO₂ accumulation alone, but the accelerating collapse of microbial ecosystems, which form the biological foundation of all planetary nutrient cycles.

Microorganisms represent the operating system of Earth’s biosphere: they drive decomposition, nutrient regeneration, soil formation, and oceanic productivity. As anthropogenic warming exceeds natural adaptation rates and chemical agriculture eliminates microbial networks, the biosphere’s core life-support functions are being dismantled. Once microbial cycles disintegrate, biodiversity collapse becomes unavoidable irrespective of atmospheric compositions.

This paper argues that a potentially viable pathway to address near-total biospheric and civilizational collapse is Direct Planetary Cooling, implemented through:
	1.	Deep-Sea Aeration — Ocean Breathing System (OBS)
	2.	Ultrasonic Mist Cooling (UMC)

Both approaches rely solely on amplification of natural physical mechanisms using existing technologies, avoiding the ecological risks inherent to chemical geoengineering schemes.

1. The Fundamental Misdiagnosis of Climate Collapse

The mainstream climate policy paradigm may be summarized as:

Global warming can be controlled primarily through reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

This framing ignores a critical reality:

Climate collapse is not a thermodynamic problem — it is an ecological systems failure.

Earth’s biosphere operates as a layered trophic structure:

Microbial decomposers
↓
Plants
↓
Insects and pollinators
↓
Herbivores
↓
Predators (including humans)

Microorganisms anchor this pyramid through:
	•	Organic matter decomposition
	•	Nutrient mineralization
	•	Soil fertility regeneration
	•	Planktonic carbon fixation
	•	Water purification

When microbial networks decay, nutrient cycling halts. All trophic layers above them inevitably fail regardless of temperature stabilization efforts undertaken decades later.

3. Historical Precedent: The End-Permian Extinction

The largest mass extinction occurred ~252 million years ago (Permian–Triassic boundary):
	•	~96% of marine species extinct
	•	~70% of terrestrial vertebrates extinct
	•	~80–90% of insect families eliminated
	•	Near-total collapse of forests
	•	Massive fungal “decomposition spikes”

Survivors consisted mainly of:
	•	Spore-forming microbes
	•	Deep-sea hydrothermal vent ecosystems
	•	Dormant and stress-resistant plant propagules

This mirrors current biological trajectories precisely — except today’s warming rate is more than 1000× faster.

Event	Temperature Rise Rate

Industrial warming	~1.5°C per century

Adaptive evolution cannot operate at this speed.

Problems with incremental approaches:
	•	CO₂ reduction does nothing to reverse existing biospheric heating
	•	Renewable energy alone addresses emissions but not accumulated thermal inertia
	•	Rewilding fails when microbial soil foundations are gone
	•	Species translocation cannot replace lost nutrient networks

Once microbial systems collapse, mitigation becomes irrelevant.

Life dies from below, not above.

6.1 Deep-Sea Aeration — Ocean Breathing System (OBS)

Mechanism
	•	Compressed air injected to ~250 m depth
	•	Spiraling nanobubbles induce vertical convection
	•	Nutrient transport from deep layers to photic zones
	•	Restoration of phytoplankton productivity
	•	Enhanced carbon sequestration
	•	Reduction of ocean surface temperature via redistributed thermal mass

Effect cascade

Vertical mixing restoration
↓
Plankton regeneration
↓
Carbon uptake ↑
↓
SST decrease
↓
Weakened ocean stratification
↓
Improved circulation → reinforcing feedback

OBS replicates the ocean’s historical convective engine which climate warming has suppressed.

6.2 Ultrasonic Mist Cooling (UMC)

Principle
	•	Ultrasonic generators produce 2–10 μm micromist
	•	Mist forms an evaporative cooling film above oceans and land surfaces
	•	Latent heat absorption up to 670 W/m²
	•	Requires only water; no chemicals or aerosols

Observed Cooling Potential
	•	Local SST reductions of 0.2–0.5°C
	•	Regional effects reaching 0.8–1.6°C
